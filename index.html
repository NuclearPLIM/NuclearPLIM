The global nuclear fleet
is ageing.

This presents challenges in a world where Nuclear Power is fundamental to meeting the energy needs of the future. But is there an opportunity for industry to modernize the existing fleet while investing in new construction and technologies to improve reliability and cost competitiveness?

What's driving the change?

Nuclear power plant construction peaked in the 1970s and declined throughout the 1990s and 2000s. The average age of the nuclear power fleet increased by 66% over the past 20 years.

Why does this matter?

Aging plants expirience issues with equipment reliability which can impact the generation produced by a plant.

Plants suffer high forced loss rate in initial break-in phase.

Afterwards, the trend of forced loss increases linearly as plants age.

Aging plants also experience an increase in planned outage durations to perform inspections and maintenance.
